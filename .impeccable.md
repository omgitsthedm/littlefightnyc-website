### Users

Little Fight NYC serves time-poor owner-operators, especially in New York City. They arrive in two main states:

- evaluating a website partner who needs to feel premium, capable, local, and easy to work with
- urgently trying to restore payments, booking, email, Wi-Fi, devices, or another customer-facing system

Assume the reader is smart but not technical. They need to understand what Little Fight does, why it matters, what proof exists, and what to do next without learning agency or software vocabulary.

### Commercial hierarchy

1. Custom local websites are the primary acquisition offer and the front door to the relationship. Do not advertise base pricing.
2. Remote and on-site support is the second path for urgent problems. Do not publish hourly pricing unless the business explicitly requests it.
3. Consulting is always free. It is the diagnostic bridge, not an equal paid product.
4. Focused custom software is the premium destination. It replaces bloated subscriptions when the workflow is specific enough to justify a build. The client owns the code, data, hosting, domain, and documentation.

The site is a lead engine. Show real work before broad claims, make websites visually dominant, keep urgent support immediately reachable, and let owned software disclose progressively as the high-value outcome.

### Brand personality

Playful, tactile, premium, and street-smart. Little Fight should feel like a sharp local partner who understands the counter, the back office, and the customer path. The emotional goals are trust, relief, curiosity, momentum, and calm competence.

### Aesthetic direction

Axiom Momentum uses a near-black canvas, NYC and shipped-work imagery, Oswald display, Barlow body, JetBrains Mono for functional labels, orange as the lead signal, blue as ambient support, 32px cards, pill controls, and precise restrained motion.

- Design variance: 8
- Motion intensity: 6
- Visual density: 4

Use asymmetric composition to communicate real priority. Avoid equal service grids, generic icon chips, repeated card scaffolding, decorative metrics, stock office imagery, and motion without a communication job.

### Design principles

1. Answer what, why, action, next, and after on every important screen.
2. Put a real proof object near the first decision.
3. Use the smallest honest commitment. The free consult should remove risk without hiding the paid work.
4. Use one Lucide icon family because the project already depends on it. Standardize stroke weight and avoid emoji.
5. Motion must communicate hierarchy, storytelling, feedback, or state. Use transform and opacity, cap ordinary transitions at 400ms, and respect reduced motion.
6. Keep marketing pages fast: prioritize the hero image only, lazy-load below-fold imagery, and avoid extra animation libraries.
7. Use plain language. Prefer website, urgent support, free consulting, and software you own over internal agency terminology.

### Foundation contracts

- New type rules use `--lf-heading`, `--lf-body`, or `--lf-mono`. The historical `--lf-serif`, `--lf-display`, and `--lf-sans` names remain compatibility aliases only.
- Page shells use the fluid `--lf-page-gutter`; readable copy uses `--lf-measure-reading`; sections, cards, and controls use their semantic fluid tokens.
- Viewport composition follows compact `<48rem`, composed `48–79.99rem`, and expansive `>=80rem`. Prefer `.lf-cq` plus `.lf-cq-grid` when a component can live in more than one shell.
- Ordinary interaction motion speaks `signal` (`180ms`) or `settle` (`320ms`) through `--m-*`. Static-first sections do not wait for a global scroll reveal.
- Interactive controls keep a `44px` minimum target and visible focus. Designs must tolerate safe-area insets, CJK line breaking, long URLs, and translated copy.
