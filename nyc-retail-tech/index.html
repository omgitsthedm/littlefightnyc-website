E-commerce platform tier ladder

Pick the platform that fits your SKU count.

10–30 SKUs, design-led

Squarespace Commerce ($23–$39/mo). Beautiful templates. Limited inventory features. Outgrown by year 2 if you scale.

50–500 SKUs, real retail

Shopify Basic ($39/mo) or Square for Retail ($0–$89/mo). Real inventory, POS sync, local pickup, multiple variants. The default for serious NYC retail.

500+ SKUs, multi-channel

Shopify ($105/mo) or Shopify Advanced ($399/mo). Heavier inventory, advanced reporting, third-party integrations.

The platform mistake: shops running a Squarespace marketing site AND a separate Shopify store with inventory in two places. Pick one platform, run everything on it. We migrate Squarespace shops to Shopify regularly when SKUs cross 50.

Retail owner FAQ

Questions NYC retail owners ask first.

Shopify or Squarespace for a NYC retail shop?

Shopify wins for any shop selling 50+ SKUs, needing inventory sync with POS, or planning to grow to thousands of products. $39/mo Basic, $105/mo Shopify, $399/mo Advanced. Squarespace wins for shops selling 10–30 SKUs that prioritize design over commerce features. $23/mo Business, $39/mo Commerce. Most NYC retail shops outgrow Squarespace in year 2.

How do I compete with Amazon Prime?

You don’t compete on price or convenience — Amazon wins those. You compete on: (1) curation (you pick the products, Amazon pushes everything), (2) experience (in-store knowledge, personal recommendations, fitting/touching), (3) speed of local pickup (faster than Amazon for in-stock), (4) loyalty (Amazon doesn’t know your name). Tech wins by making local pickup obvious on the website and reminding customers you’re 4 blocks away.

What about Square POS for retail?

Square for Retail starts at $0/mo (with 2.6% + 10¢ per transaction) for solo shops, $89/mo Plus for multiple staff and inventory features. Integrates with Square Online (free with paid Square POS plan) for e-commerce. Best for shops with under ~500 SKUs.

Do I need both a website and an e-commerce platform?

Most NYC retail shops only need one. Shopify and Squarespace both have website-builder features built in, so you don’t need a separate WordPress site. The mistake we see: shops paying for a Squarespace site AND a separate Shopify store, with inventory in two places. Pick one platform, run everything on it.

How does local pickup work for a NYC shop?

Customer orders online, gets a "ready to pickup in 30 minutes" email/SMS, walks in to grab it. The platform (Shopify or Square Online) handles the order; you handle the pull-and-pack. Done right, this beats Amazon Prime for in-stock items because customers can have it in 30 minutes instead of 2 hours.
