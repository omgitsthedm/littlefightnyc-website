Software guide

Airtable vs Notion vs Monday for small business operations.

Airtable is usually strongest when the business needs structured records, views, forms, and light database behavior. Notion is strongest for notes, documentation, and flexible team knowledge. Monday can be useful for task-heavy teams that need status tracking, assignments, and repeatable project boards.

Local context

Why New York changes the decision.

Small New York teams often do not need an enterprise platform. They need the place where leads, jobs, tasks, follow-up, and owner visibility stop living in five disconnected places.

The daily work first. The software second.

The best tool is the one that supports how customers find you, how staff capture the action, and how the work moves after that.

Keep / Connect / Replace / Build

Do not buy software before you check the fit.

Keep

What works

Keep the tool your team actually updates. Keep simple docs or boards when they are working.

Connect

What matters

Connect website forms, email, tasks, reminders, and owner views.

Replace

What drags

Replace scattered spreadsheets that nobody trusts. Replace tools that add admin without improving decisions.

Build

What fits

Build an intake-to-task workflow. Build owner owner views for leads, jobs, revenue signals, and open loops.

FAQ

Fast answers.

Is Airtable a customer list?

It can behave like a lightweight customer list if the workflow is simple and configured well.

Is Notion enough for operations?

Notion is great for knowledge and notes, but structured tracking often needs more discipline.

When should we build custom?

When the process is specific, repeated, and awkward across generic tools.
