Software guide

Custom business system vs another SaaS subscription.

Use SaaS when the workflow is standard, the tool solves most of the problem, integrations are mature, support matters, and the monthly cost is justified by real usage. Consider a custom or lightweight system when the business uses only a small part of an expensive platform, staff work around the software, data lives in too many places, or reporting is manual.

Local context

Why New York changes the decision.

The real cost of software is not just the subscription. It is also staff time, double entry, missed leads, bad reporting, confusing onboarding, software overlap, and the workaround everyone promises to fix later.

Workflow first. Software second.

The best tool is the one that supports how customers find you, how staff capture the action, and how the work moves after that.
