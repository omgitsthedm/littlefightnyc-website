When each wins

Honest read on which fits.

Free theme + apps wins if:

  • You’re testing the business idea — under 30 SKUs, under $5K/month revenue
  • You don’t mind looking like the next 50,000 stores
  • You’re fine with $200–$500/month in app fees indefinitely
  • You’re technically fine with managing 10+ app dashboards
  • Mobile speed isn’t a priority for your customers

Custom Shopify theme wins if:

  • Your store does $10K+/month and the app stack is eating margin
  • You want a brand that doesn’t look like every Dawn store
  • Mobile speed and Google rankings matter to your acquisition
  • You want one place to manage everything (Shopify admin)
  • You want a real human to call when something breaks at 7pm Friday

Owner FAQ

Questions retail owners ask first.

Is Shopify good for a NYC small retail business?

Yes — Shopify itself is excellent. The trap is using a free theme (Dawn, Sense, Crave) and stacking 10–15 paid apps to make basic features work. That’s where the cost compounds. A custom-built Shopify theme or a custom storefront often costs less over 5 years than the app stack on top of a free theme.

What’s the Shopify app stack problem?

Free Shopify themes are intentionally limited so you’ll buy apps. Typical NYC retail Shopify store ends up with: review app ($15/mo), filter/search app ($20/mo), product bundle app ($25/mo), reviews + UGC app ($30/mo), shipping calculator ($10/mo), abandoned-cart email ($15/mo), pop-up app ($10/mo), inventory sync ($30/mo), loyalty app ($30/mo), wishlist app ($10/mo). That’s $195/month — $11,700 over 5 years — on top of Shopify’s $39–$399 plan.

What does a Shopify storefront really cost over 5 years?

Shopify Basic ($39/mo) + typical app stack ($150–$250/mo) = $190–$290/month, or $11,400–$17,400 over 5 years. Plus 2.9% transaction fees. Plus theme updates that break apps. A custom-coded theme reduces app dependency and pays for itself in 18–24 months. See the lifetime cost calculator for your specific tier.

Does Google rank Shopify template stores well?

Shopify itself is fine for SEO. Free themes loaded with 12+ apps inject so much JavaScript that Lighthouse Performance drops to 30–50 on mobile, and Core Web Vitals fail. Google notices. Custom Shopify themes (or alternative custom storefronts) hit 90+ Lighthouse and rank measurably better.

Should I leave Shopify entirely?

Usually no. Shopify’s checkout, payment processing, and inventory sync are best-in-class for retail. The fix is a custom theme that eliminates 60–80% of the app stack — not leaving Shopify.
