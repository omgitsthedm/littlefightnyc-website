Owner FAQ

Questions owners ask first.

How long does a small business website take?

14 days, hero to launch. If we’re not live by day 14, you don’t pay. Most owners are surprised at how fast this is — agencies typically quote 8-12 weeks for the same scope.

How much does a NYC small business website cost?

From $2,400 flat for a focused 5-page site. $4,000-$6,000 for larger sites with booking, e-commerce, or custom integrations. Hosting + care: $25/month. No monthly trap, no surprise bills.

What’s included in the $2,400 baseline?

5 pages: home, services, about, contact, plus one specific page for your business (menu, booking, gallery, etc.). Mobile-first design, copywriting collaboration, real photos, schema, GA4, contact form. Domain + 1 year hosting included.

What if I want changes after launch?

You own the site outright. Edits cost the same as our hourly: $150/hr. Most small change batches: 1-2 hours. We don’t charge a monthly retainer just for occasional updates.

Will my site rank on Google?

We build with current SEO and AEO best practices: clean schema, fast loading, mobile-first, real content. But ranking depends on your competition and ongoing work. We’ll tell you honestly what your site can realistically rank for.

Why is this faster than agencies?

No account managers. No 4-tier proposal process. One human who builds. We start with a Fit Check, scope it together, then ship. Agencies bill for meetings about the meeting; we bill for the build.
