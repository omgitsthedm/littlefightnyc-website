# Little Fight NYC Brand Kit

Version 2.0.0
Updated July 24, 2026
Owner: hello@littlefightnyc.com

This is the operating guide for the Little Fight NYC brand. The live visual
guide is at https://littlefightnyc.com/brand-kit/.

## Brand core

**Purpose:** Give small businesses the technology advantage that usually comes
with a much bigger team.

**Positioning:** Little Fight NYC is a small, hands-on NYC technology firm for
owner-operated businesses that need a custom website, broken technology fixed,
or focused software without enterprise bloat.

**Promise:** The technology fits the business. The business does not bend
around the technology.

**Fight:** Bloat, lock-in, broken workflows, template limits, vague vendors,
and technology that gets in the way of the day.

**House line:** Small crew. Heavy pull.

**Core story:** Small businesses have enough to fight. Their technology should
not be one.

## Audience states

### Urgent owner

Something is broken now. Lead with calm, a direct path to help, and the next
useful action.

### Evaluating owner

They are deciding if Little Fight is capable enough to trust. Lead with shipped
work, ownership, a clear process, and verified proof.

## Narrative

Every important page follows the same movement:

1. **Name the fight:** The website should bring business. The tools should save
   time. The technology should work.
2. **Cut the drag:** Fix what is broken. Connect what works. Replace only what
   slows the business down.
3. **Leave ownership:** The client owns the code, data, domain, hosting, and
   documentation.

## Messaging pillars

### Be found

Custom websites built for speed, search, local relevance, and the way customers
choose.

### Keep moving

Bring us the broken booking, payments, email, devices, Wi-Fi, or website. We
diagnose first and fix the right thing.

### Cut the drag

Keep useful tools, connect what should talk, and stop paying for software that
creates more work.

### Own what fits

When generic software stops fitting, build the focused system the business
actually needs.

## Voice

Plain, confident, warm, street-smart, and on the side of the owner.

Use:

- Your business has outgrown the template.
- Show us what is broken.
- Stop renting the workaround.
- Small crew. Heavy pull.
- We make the technology fit the business.

Avoid:

- Seamless digital solutions for modern enterprises.
- Wix is bad for Google.
- Guaranteed first-page rankings.
- Replace every tool with one platform.
- Strategy jargon, fear, vague superlatives, and unverified numbers.

Comparisons must stay honest. Template platforms can be appropriate. Little
Fight earns the work through better fit, ownership, performance, customer-path
control, and direct support.

## Identity

### Mark

The Tugboat represents a small craft pulling above its size.

- Clear space: at least one smokestack on every side.
- Minimum size: 20px for favicons, 32px everywhere else.
- Use the supplied white, near-black, or orange fixed-color file.
- Use `mark-currentcolor.svg` only when the SVG is inline and CSS must control
  its color.
- Never stretch, rotate, outline, shadow, add a gradient, or redraw the mark.
- Never place it over a busy image without a solid ground or strong scrim.

The named white, near-black, and orange files contain fixed fills so they stay
reliable in design tools and when opened directly. They are not `currentColor`
assets.

### Color

| Role | Value | Token |
|---|---:|---|
| Primary ground | `#050507` | `--lf-ink` |
| Elevated surface | `#1A1C23` | `--lf-paper` |
| Recessed surface | `#12141A` | `--lf-paper-2` |
| Border | `#27272A` | `--lf-hairline` |
| Primary text | `#FFFFFF` | `--lf-bone` |
| Secondary text | `#A1A1AA` | `--lf-bone-soft` |
| Lead signal | `#F97316` | `--lf-fight` |
| Supporting accent | `#3B82F6` | `--lf-blue` |

Orange means act, live, or the point. Blue supports. Red means fault. Green
means confirmed. Do not use orange as decoration.

### Typography

- **Oswald Variable 700:** Headlines, prominent names, and the wordmark.
- **Barlow 400 and 700:** Reading copy, lists, and controls.
- **JetBrains Mono 500 and 700:** Functional labels, dates, status, and verified
  facts.

Use a 16px absolute floor. Body copy normally sits at 18px to 20px.

### Shape

- Small details: 12px
- Controls: 32px
- Cards and panels: 32px
- Pills: 9999px

## Imagery

Show the place where work happens:

- storefronts, counters, menus, shelves, product, inventory, devices, cables,
  screens, and back-office systems
- real constraints shown with dignity
- people absent when possible
- if people appear, keep them incidental, candid, distant, or softly out of
  focus

Do not use generic offices, staged laptop teams, AI faces, empty skyline
cliches, glossy 3D filler, or images that turn a small business into a disaster
scene.

## Motion

1. **Many into one:** Scattered tools become one calm system.
2. **Weight before flash:** Objects pull, settle, and follow through.
3. **Static first paint:** The complete page is available before enhancement.
4. **Reward, never tax:** No splash, scroll jail, cursor follower, or required
   animation.
5. **Reduced motion:** Every effect has a calm static state.

Ordinary feedback uses 180ms signal and 320ms settle timing. Animate transform
and opacity.

## Proof and claims

Approved standing claims:

- Custom small-business websites go live in 14 days, or the client does not
  pay.
- Consulting is always free.
- Callback target is within 2 hours, 9am to 9pm Eastern Time.
- Clients own their code, data, domain, hosting, and documentation.
- On-site support covers all five NYC boroughs. Websites are available
  nationwide.

Every claim needs a source, owner, last-verified date, and approved placements.
Never invent project dates, outcomes, percentages, testimonials, or current
status. Label reconstructed timelines clearly.

## Current-work standard

Create the public project record when the deposit clears.

Milestones:

1. Deposit received
2. Discovery
3. Working proof
4. Owner review and training
5. Live
6. Care and iteration

Every record shows the start date, current milestone, latest completed work,
next milestone, days in motion, last-verified date, public or private status,
and client approval.

Never expose private systems, credentials, source, customer data, internal
discussion, or details the client has not approved.

## Lab showcase contract

The Lab is a showroom, not developer documentation.

- One click at most from the Lab index to the live experience.
- Deliver something useful or delightful within ten seconds.
- Put replay, reset, context, and the next exhibit beside the experience.
- Do not create a nested reading maze.
- Never surface GitHub, repository names, source code, copy code, share code,
  download code, commit hashes, file trees, package details, schemas, or
  private client infrastructure.

## Accessibility

- 44px minimum target size
- visible keyboard focus
- WCAG-aware contrast
- meaning never carried by color alone
- useful alt text
- logical reading order
- support for long translations, CJK text, long URLs, and 200 percent zoom
- reduced-motion support
- complete content without JavaScript

## Governance

1. Design direction: `app/DESIGN.md`
2. Canonical design tokens: `app/src/styles/editorial/tokens.css`
3. Public operating guide and assets: `/brand-kit/`
4. Machine-readable copy: `assets/color/tokens.json`

If these conflict, update the Brand Kit from the canonical design files. Do not
create a second palette or type system in another tool.
