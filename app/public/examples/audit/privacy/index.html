Privacy

We only ask for what the audit needs.

The Website Audit Lab asks for a public website URL and an email address for the report. Do not send passwords, private documents, payment details, recovery codes, or account access through this audit.

What we collect

  • The public website URL submitted for the audit.
  • The email address used to send the report.
  • Technical audit results for the submitted public page.

How we use it

The email address is used to send the audit report and, when appropriate, follow up about fixing the issues found. Little Fight NYC does not sell audit submissions.

What the audit does not need

The Website Audit Lab does not need logins, passwords, recovery codes, payment details, private files, customer records, staff records, API keys, or access to business accounts. It reviews public website information and technical signals that are already visible from the outside. If a deeper project later requires access to a website, Google profile, hosting account, booking tool, or payment platform, Little Fight will walk through a safer access method outside this public form.

Report delivery and follow-up

The report is sent to the email address provided. Little Fight may use that email to follow up about the report, ask a practical clarification, or suggest the free Tech Audit when the page problem appears connected to a larger website, search, software, lead, booking, payment, or workflow issue. The point is clarity, not pressure.

Public website testing

The submitted page may be tested for speed, mobile experience, search basics, accessibility signals, trust cues, and conversion friction. The report is not a legal, financial, security, or compliance certification. It is a fast first read designed to help a business owner decide whether a focused fix, a website cleanup, IT support, local search help, or a broader systems review makes sense.
