Restaurant owner FAQ

Questions NYC restaurant owners ask first.

What tech does a NYC restaurant actually need?

Six things: a POS that handles your service style (Square for fast/casual, Toast for full-service), a website that converts (menu, hours, reservation link, online ordering), reservations (Resy or Tock), online ordering (Toast Delivery, Square Online, ChowNow), Wi-Fi that doesn’t drop during rush, and someone to call when any of it breaks. Most NYC restaurants only need 2–3 of those done well — the rest can wait.

Square or Toast for a NYC restaurant?

Square for cafes, bars, takeout, pop-ups. Toast for full-service restaurants with complex menus, kitchen routing, multi-role staff. Square is $0/mo + 2.6% transaction fee; Toast is $69–$165/month per terminal plus proprietary hardware. Most NYC owners we audit are on Toast when Square would do.

How much does a NYC restaurant website cost?

Independent shops (like Little Fight) come in around $2,000–$5,000 for a real working restaurant site with menu, reservation embed, online ordering integration, and mobile-first design. NYC agencies charge $5,000–$15,000+. DIY on Squarespace is $200–$500/year but takes 30+ hours of your time.

What about Wi-Fi for the dinner rush?

The most common NYC restaurant POS issue we fix: Wi-Fi crashes at peak. Cause is usually guest Wi-Fi and POS sharing the same network/band. Fix: separate guest VLAN, lock POS to 5GHz with a wired backup. Takes 2–3 hours on-site. Stops the issue permanently.

2-hour response during NYC business hours (9am–9pm Eastern). After hours, our 24-hour live AI phone agent answers (646) 360-0318 — captures the issue and routes urgent calls to David. For most restaurant POS issues, that combination beats the GEEK-AID/MSP model.
