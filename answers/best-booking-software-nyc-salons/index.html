Salon booking

Best booking software for a NYC salon? Square Appointments or GlossGenius for most. Boulevard for high-end.

Solo to 5 stylists: Square Appointments ($0 base + 2.6%) or GlossGenius ($24/mo, slick UI). 5+ stylists or membership-driven: Boulevard ($175+/mo, real). Most NYC salons over-pay.

Direct answer

Solo to 5 stylists: Square Appointments or GlossGenius. 5+ chairs with memberships: Boulevard. Most NYC salons we audit are on a tier they don’t need.

For a NYC salon, the right booking software depends on size and complexity, not features. Solo to 5 stylists, simple bookings: Square Appointments ($0/mo base + 2.6% transaction fee, free hardware promo, simple), or GlossGenius ($24/mo flat, slicker UI built for salons, payment processing baked in). Both handle online booking, deposits, no-show fees, reminders, basic client records, and Instagram booking links. 5+ stylists with memberships, packages, or detailed staff commission rules: Boulevard ($175+/mo per location), Vagaro ($30+/mo per stylist), or Mindbody for the wellness/multi-service crowd ($129+/mo). Don’t use: generic calendars (Calendly, Acuity solo) for a salon — you’ll outgrow them in a quarter and the migration is painful. Watch for the upgrade trap: most salons get pushed to a $175/mo tier when GlossGenius would do the job for $24. Run the math against actual booking volume before committing — we audit this regularly for NYC salons (first consulting hour is free).

  • Solo to 5 stylists, no memberships → Square Appointments ($0 + 2.6%) or GlossGenius ($24/mo)
  • 5+ stylists, packages, memberships → Boulevard ($175+/mo) or Vagaro ($30/stylist)
  • Wellness/spa with classes → Mindbody ($129+/mo)
  • Avoid generic calendars (Calendly, Acuity solo) for salons — built for one-on-one consults, not chairs
  • Most NYC salons we audit are on a tier they don’t need — check your math before another renewal

Questions owners ask next.

For many small NYC salons, Square Appointments or GlossGenius can be enough if booking, payments, reminders, and simple client records are the core need. Larger salons may need deeper staff calendars, deposits, memberships, inventory, or reporting. The best choice is the one that reduces missed appointments and admin work without adding a bill nobody understands.
How many stylists or providers need calendars? Do deposits, cancellation rules, or memberships matter? Do clients book from Instagram, Google, or the website? What reminders prevent no-shows? What data does the owner need weekly?
