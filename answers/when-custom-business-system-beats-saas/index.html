Custom vs SaaS

When does custom-built software beat another SaaS subscription?

When you pay $200+/month for tools your staff still works around. When 2–3 apps need to share data and don’t. When the next SaaS tier is 5× the price for one feature you need. That’s when custom pays back.

By David Marsh · Published May 6, 2026

Direct answer

Custom wins when you pay $200+/month and staff still works around it. SaaS wins when the workflow is standard and the team uses the whole tool.

Custom-built software beats SaaS when four things are true: (1) you pay over $200/month for a tool, (2) staff still works around it (spreadsheets, paper, manual copy), (3) two or more apps need to share data and don’t, or (4) the next SaaS tier is 5× the price for one feature you actually need. Below those thresholds, SaaS almost always wins — you don’t need to build what already exists. The 3-year math: a $200/month SaaS tool costs $7,200 over 3 years. A focused custom build runs $4,000–$8,000 once, plus $50–$200/mo hosting. Custom pays back inside year 2 if you commit. The trap: most owners who think they need custom actually just need to downgrade a SaaS tier or kill duplicate tools. The audit costs $1,500; the wrong build costs $10,000. Get the audit first if you’re not sure.

  • SaaS over $200/mo + staff workarounds → custom probably worth it
  • SaaS under $100/mo + everyone uses it → keep SaaS
  • Two apps need to share data and don’t → custom or integration
  • You’ve outgrown a SaaS but the next tier is 5× → build instead
  • 3-year cost is the right comparison, not monthly

A custom or lightweight business system may beat SaaS when the business only uses a small part of an expensive platform, staff keep working around the software, data lives in too many places, and the same manual workflow happens every week. SaaS still wins when the workflow is standard, compliance is high, support is mature, and the team uses most of the tool.
What is the three-year subscription cost? What work still happens outside the platform? How many seats are paid for but barely used? Does the workflow need a platform or one clean path? Would a smaller build reduce admin hours?
