POS freezing

POS keeps freezing during NYC rush. Here’s how to find the culprit fast.

95% of the time it’s Wi-Fi, a recent update, or a peripheral conflict. 1–3 hour fix once we’re on-site. Don’t lose another rush hour to it.

Call (646) 360-0318Or start a Fit Check
By David Marsh · Published May 6, 2026

Direct answer

95% of the time it’s one of: Wi-Fi drops at peak, a recent firmware update, or a printer/card-reader conflict. 1–3 hour fix on-site.

When a NYC POS keeps freezing — Square, Toast, Clover, doesn’t matter — the cause is almost always one of three things: (1) Wi-Fi drops during peak hours because the guest network and POS are on the same band/channel, customers connect, bandwidth crashes. Fix: separate guest VLAN, lock POS to 5GHz. (2) Recent firmware/iOS update introduced incompatibility — common after iPad updates. Fix: roll back the update or update the POS app. (3) Peripheral conflict — printer, card reader, cash drawer fighting for USB or Bluetooth. Fix: power-cycle each in sequence, identify the bad actor. Less common: faulty hardware (replace), bad cable, low-power outlet, expired SSL certs in the POS app. Diagnostic takes 30 minutes; fix takes 1–3 hours total. We do this same-day for NYC shops — call before the next rush starts.

  • Freezes during peak hours → Wi-Fi network conflict (most common)
  • Started after an iPad/Android update → firmware/app incompatibility
  • Hangs after printing or card swipe → peripheral conflict
  • Random freezes, no pattern → could be hardware, cable, or SSL
  • Affects only one station → that device’s storage/RAM full or app crash

Questions owners ask next.

If a POS keeps freezing, check the network, device health, software updates, payment reader, printer connection, and whether the issue happens at certain times or stations. If it affects customers or payments right now, treat it as urgent support first and a deeper setup review second.
Does it happen on one device or every station? Does Wi-Fi drop at the same time? Are printers or card readers involved? Did an update or hardware change happen recently? Is the team using workarounds during rush?
