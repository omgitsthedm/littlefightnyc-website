Restaurant POS

Square or Toast for a NYC restaurant? Square wins for most. Toast wins for full-service.

Cafes, bars, pop-ups, takeout-heavy spots: Square. Full-service with complex menus, table management, kitchen routing, multi-role staff: Toast. The cost difference matters more than NYC owners think.

By David Marsh · Published May 6, 2026

Direct answer

Square for cafes, bars, takeout, pop-ups. Toast for full-service with complex menus or kitchen workflow. The cost gap is real: $0–$60/mo vs $69–$165/mo plus hardware lock-in.

For most NYC restaurants under 50 covers, Square is the right call: $0/month base (free hardware promo, just card processing fees ~2.6% + 10¢), works on iPad you already own, simple menu setup, no contract. Square fits cafes, bars, takeout-heavy spots, pop-ups, food halls, and most casual dining. Toast costs $69–$165/month per terminal plus proprietary hardware ($800+ per station), but earns it back on full-service operations: complex menus, table management, kitchen display systems (KDS), waitstaff roles, deep reporting. The wrong choice is real money: a 2-station bar pays $200–$400/month extra on Toast it doesn’t need; a 30-table restaurant on Square loses time on every cover because table management is bolted on. Most NYC owners we audit are on Toast when Square would do, paying for compliance and reporting features they don’t use.

  • Square fits: cafes, bars, takeout, pop-ups, casual under 50 covers
  • Toast fits: full-service, complex menus, KDS, multi-role staff, 50+ covers
  • Square cost: $0/mo + 2.6% + 10¢ per card transaction
  • Toast cost: $69–$165/mo per terminal + $800+ proprietary hardware + similar processing fees
  • Switching cost is real: 1–2 weeks to migrate menu, retrain staff, reconnect online ordering
