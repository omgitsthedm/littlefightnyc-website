Case studies

Examples framed around relief, not a sales pitch.

01

Manhattan service business

Problem: inquiries landed in multiple places and follow-up depended on memory.

  • Kept: working contact habits.
  • Connected: website form, owner notification, lead sheet, and follow-up.
  • Built: one view for open leads.
  • Changed: the owner could see new inquiries in one place.
  • Tools: website form, Google Workspace, lead table, analytics.
02

Local hospitality brand

Problem: the public site did not explain the offer quickly enough and direct action was unclear.

  • Kept: brand warmth and existing tools.
  • Connected: service pages, calls, maps, booking/payment path.
  • Replaced: vague page copy and weak buttons.
  • Changed: customers had a clearer path to act.
  • Tools: static site, Google profile, booking/payment link, analytics.
03

Creative studio follow-up

Problem: estimates, approvals, and project details were scattered.

  • Kept: the studio's working project language.
  • Connected: inquiry, qualification, estimate draft, and owner review.
  • Built: a lightweight proposal path.
  • Changed: less manual copying and fewer open loops.
  • Tools: website, forms, Google Workspace, simple tracker.
