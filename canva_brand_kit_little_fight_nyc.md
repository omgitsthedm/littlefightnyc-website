# Canva Brand Kit: Little Fight NYC

Version 2.0.0
Updated July 24, 2026

This is the Canva translation of the canonical Little Fight NYC system. Do not
create a separate Canva palette or type direction.

## Brand core

- Purpose: Give small businesses the technology advantage that usually comes
  with a much bigger team.
- Promise: The technology fits the business.
- House line: Small crew. Heavy pull.
- Mark: The Tugboat.
- Voice: Plain, confident, warm, street-smart, and on the side of the owner.

use orange as decoration.

## Canva typography

- Headings: Oswald Bold, weight 700
- Body: Barlow Regular, weight 400
- Body emphasis: Barlow Bold, weight 700
- Functional labels: JetBrains Mono Medium or Bold

Use 16px or its Canva equivalent as the absolute type floor. Keep reading copy
simple and large enough for quick scanning.

## Logo

Upload the supplied fixed-color assets:

- `mark-white.svg`
- `mark-ink.svg`
- `mark-orange.svg`
- horizontal and stacked lockups for white, near-black, and orange grounds

Do not upload `mark-currentcolor.svg` to Canva. That file is for inline web use.

Clear space is at least one smokestack on every side. Never stretch, rotate,
outline, shadow, recolor, or add a gradient.

## Image searches and uploads

Prioritize real or approved photographs of:

- neighborhood storefronts
- counters and point-of-sale equipment
- menus, shelves, and product
- inventory and work surfaces
- routers, devices, cables, and back-office systems

Avoid generic offices, staged laptop teams, AI faces, glossy abstract objects,
and empty skyline cliches. Keep people absent when possible. If present, they
should be incidental, candid, distant, or softly out of focus.

## Layout

- Use a near-black page with 32px cards and pill controls.
- Keep orange for the primary action or the point.
- Use blue as a supporting cue, not a second primary color.
- Prefer one strong visual and one short statement over dense text.
- Avoid equal feature-card walls and repeated icon boxes.

## Motion for Canva video

- Many things consolidate into one.
- Objects move with weight and settle.
- Keep ordinary transitions short.
- Never use bounce, cursor effects, splash screens, or motion that delays the
  message.

## Copy examples

Use:

- Your business has outgrown the template.
- Show us what is broken.
- Stop renting the workaround.
- Small crew. Heavy pull.

Avoid:

- Seamless digital solutions.
- Wix is bad for Google.
- Guaranteed rankings.
- Unverified numbers or invented client outcomes.

## Export checklist

1. Confirm the correct fixed-color logo file.
2. Confirm orange is the only lead signal.
3. Confirm body copy is readable at a glance.
4. Confirm imagery shows the business, not a generic team.
5. Confirm no claim or project status is invented.
6. Confirm the footer line when the format allows it:
   `Designed, Hosted and Cared For by LittleFightNYC.com`
