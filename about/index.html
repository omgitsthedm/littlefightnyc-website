Why I started this

A pharmacy shouldn’t need corporate IT.

I’ve spent years inside small businesses watching the same fight: a salon paying for booking software that loses appointments. A shop’s POS that double-charges customers because someone updated firmware. A dentist whose website went down on a Friday and stayed down for a week because his agency disappeared.

Big companies have whole IT departments to handle this. Local shops have me. I built Little Fight to be the technology partner that small businesses actually deserve — one human, real time, fair pricing, no monthly contracts that lock you in. Helped 20+ NYC businesses grow profit since 2017. 100+ nationwide since 2012.

I work primarily in Manhattan and surrounding boroughs but take remote work nationwide. The four things I do — websites, custom systems, consulting, IT support — cover most of what an owner-operated business needs from a tech partner. If I’m not the right help, I’ll tell you who is.

What I believe

Three things I won’t budge on.

1. Plain English first

You should never need a glossary to ask me for help. If I use a tech word, I owe you a translation.

2. The bill has to earn it

If a tool doesn’t save time, win customers, or protect trust, it has no business charging you every month.

3. Local businesses are worth defending

A good neighborhood business shouldn’t lose to a chain because the chain has cleaner tools. That’s the fight.

How I work

Keep what works. Connect what matters. Replace what drags. Build what fits.

Most owners come to me with a hunch — "we’re paying too much" or "the website’s fine but leads still die." My first move is always the smallest useful one. Sometimes that’s a 2-hour fix. Sometimes that’s a 30-day audit. Sometimes that’s a custom build over 60 days. I tell you which one you actually need, even when it’s the cheapest one.
