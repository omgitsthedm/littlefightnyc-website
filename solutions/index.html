From monthly drag to local advantage

Most tech problems are small leaks.

A weak page. A broken form. A tool nobody uses. A spreadsheet everyone needs. We stop the leaks that cost money, customers, time, and trust.

By David Marsh · Published May 5, 2026

Thesis

Get seen. Get trusted. Get paid. Follow up.

Google brings people in. The website earns trust. The tools take action. Follow-up turns interest into business.

  • A good-looking site still leaks if the form goes nowhere.
  • More Google traffic becomes pressure if follow-up is scattered.
  • Booking does not help if the offer is unclear.
  • If staff still use spreadsheets, the expensive tool is not doing its job.

Framework

Keep. Cut. Connect. Build.

Keep

What helps

Good tools, useful pages, and helpful habits stay.

Cut

What drains money

Tools go when they cost too much or make the day harder.

Connect

What should talk

Working pieces get tied together so leads stop wandering off.

Build

The missing advantage

We build the missing piece when the monthly tool cannot do it cleanly.

Rollout

A 30 / 60 / 90 path that respects real life.

30 days: stop the leak

Fix broken paths, clarify the next step, clean up Google, and make customers reachable.

60 days: lower the drag

Connect forms, booking, payments, email, and lead tracking.

90 days: build or replace

Replace bad-fit tools only after the real daily pattern is clear.

Next move

Not a teardown. A sharper refit.

We start with the bill, the lost customers, and the manual work. Fewer loose ends. Better Tuesdays.
