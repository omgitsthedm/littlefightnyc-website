From software drag to a system that fits

Most businesses do not have one big tech problem. They have a lot of tiny leaks.

A weak page here. A clunky form there. A booking tool doing half the job. A spreadsheet with trust issues. We make the digital side of the business fit the real one.

Start With a Fit CheckOpen business systems
By David Marsh · Updated May 5, 2026

Thesis

Get seen. Get chosen. Get organized. Get better.

Local search brings people in. The website earns trust. The tools capture the action. The system moves the work forward. If any piece breaks, the business leaks opportunity. We look at the whole chain without coating the work in consulting fog.

  • If the site looks good but the form goes nowhere, leads still get lost.
  • If SEO works but follow-up is scattered, traffic becomes pressure.
  • If booking works but the offer is unclear, customers hesitate.
  • If staff still use spreadsheets and memory, the system is not really working.

Framework

Keep / Connect / Replace / Build. No drama. Just fit.

Keep

Useful tools

Good software, useful pages, and sane habits stay when they are worth the cost.

Connect

Isolated pieces

Working tools get tied together so data and follow-up stop wandering off.

Replace

Misfit software

Tools go when they are expensive, rigid, underused, or quietly bossing the business around.

Build

Missing systems

We build the dashboard, intake flow, workflow, or page that off-the-shelf tools do not handle well.

Rollout

A 30 / 60 / 90 rollout that respects reality.

30 days: stabilize

Fix broken paths, clarify the CTA, repair tracking, clean up the Google profile, and make the next move visible.

60 days: connect

Connect forms, booking, payments, email, CRM/database, dashboards, and reporting where they matter.

90 days: build or replace

Replace bad-fit tools or build the missing workflow only after the real operating pattern is clear.

Next move

Not a teardown. A sharper refit.

We start with how work actually moves through the business, then build the digital pieces around that. Fewer loose ends. Fewer heroic workarounds. Better Tuesdays.
