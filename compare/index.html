Website builders

DIY platforms vs hand-built.

Little Fight vs Squarespace

The most common alternative for NYC small biz websites. Squarespace wins for solo operators with no integrations and time to learn the platform. Little Fight wins when the site has to convert customers, integrate with POS or booking, or be owned outright.

See full comparison →

Little Fight vs Wix

Wix is fine for hobbyists and side projects. The Wix tax (Editor lock-in, slow mobile, generic templates, app upsells) compounds fast for any business doing real volume. Migration off Wix is rough — build right the first time.

See full comparison →

Little Fight vs GoDaddy

GoDaddy is a price floor, not a quality floor. Cheap upfront, but the support is famously bad, the templates are dated, and the upsells never stop. We’d rather see you on Squarespace if you’re DIY-ing.

See full comparison →

Little Fight vs WordPress

WordPress.org with a custom theme is fine if you have a developer. WordPress.com plus 15–30 plugins is plugin-rot waiting to happen. Custom outperforms typical WordPress on every metric for most NYC small biz.

Templates & marketplaces

When the platform is good but the template traps you.

Little Fight vs Shopify Templates

Shopify itself is excellent for retail. Free Shopify themes plus 12 paid apps to make basic features work is the trap — $11K+ over 5 years. Stay on Shopify; replace the free theme.

See full comparison →

Little Fight vs Webflow Templates

Webflow is solid — clean code, decent SEO. The $79 templates lock you into someone else’s grid; customizing them costs more than a custom build would have. Custom Webflow or hand-coded both beat templates.

See full comparison →

Little Fight vs Fiverr

Fiverr’s $50 website is a free template repackaged. Sold to 200 other small businesses. Won’t rank, won’t convert, the seller vanishes in 6 months. Real 5-year cost is $1,500–$3,000 once you count fixes and lost customers.

See full comparison →

IT support

NYC IT shops vs Little Fight.

Little Fight vs GEEK-AID

The strongest direct NYC IT competitor. GEEK-AID wins for 10+ employee shops wanting unlimited monthly IT at $89.95/employee. Little Fight wins for owner-operated shops needing IT alongside web/systems — and who want one human, not a helpdesk.

See full comparison →

Little Fight vs other NYC MSPs

Power Consulting, WheelHouse IT, Optistar, Diligex, and others target mid-market enterprises ($150–$250/user/mo). Great for 50+ staff with complex IT needs. Almost always wrong for owner-operated NYC small biz. Call us first — we’ll refer you to the right MSP if you’re bigger than us.

The honest meta

When NOT to hire Little Fight.

Pick Squarespace if

You’re solo, no integrations, the site just has to exist, and you have 30+ hours to build it yourself. Don’t pay us for that.

Pick GEEK-AID if

You have 10+ employees, predictable IT load, want a fixed $89.95/employee monthly bill, and need 24/7 human emergency response.

Pick a real MSP if

You’re 50+ staff with enterprise infrastructure (Active Directory, Exchange Server). We’ll refer you to Power Consulting or WheelHouse IT.
