Plain English

IT support is not the goal. A business day that works is the goal.

Most owners do not wake up wanting "managed services." They want the register to work, the team to get online, the printer to stop wasting time, the email to send, the booking tool to behave, and the customer not to notice anything was wrong.

  • Something is broken right now.
  • The same tech problem keeps coming back.
  • Staff waste time on workarounds.
  • One person knows how everything is connected.
  • The monthly support bill is bigger than the actual help.

What we fix

Support for the things that block customers, staff, or money.

Wi-Fi and networks

Coverage problems, unstable office or storefront networks, guest Wi-Fi, router cleanup, and equipment that needs to stop guessing.

POS and payments

Square, Toast-adjacent setups, iPad registers, receipt printers, card readers, network reliability, and payment paths that need to hold up.

Devices and accounts

Mac, PC, iPad, iPhone, Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, shared access, onboarding, offboarding, and ownership cleanup.

Web, email, and forms

Domains, DNS, email routing, website forms, booking embeds, notifications, and the quiet setup pieces that lose leads when they fail.

How we route it

Urgent first. Cleanup second. No drama if a small fix will do.

01

Is it affecting customers, payments, booking, email, access, or staff right now?

02

03

04

Prevent repeat pain
