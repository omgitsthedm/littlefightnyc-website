When each wins

Honest read on which is right for you.

Pick Squarespace if:

  • You’re a solo operator with one offering
  • The site doesn’t need to integrate with anything specific
  • You have 30+ hours to learn the platform and build it
  • You’re fine with a template that looks like other small businesses
  • You’re OK paying $192–$588/year forever

Pick Little Fight if:

  • You need it live in 14 days, not 6 months
  • The site has to integrate with POS, booking, or a custom system
  • You want to own the site outright — no monthly trap
  • You want a real human to call when something breaks
  • You’ve already tried Squarespace and the site still doesn’t convert

Owner FAQ

Questions owners ask first.

Should a NYC small business use Squarespace or hire Little Fight?

Squarespace wins for solo operators with no time to manage anything custom — pure DIY at $16–$49/month. Little Fight wins when the site has to actually convert customers, integrate with POS or booking, or be owned outright with no platform lock-in. Most NYC owners who try Squarespace for 6 months and still don’t have a working site end up calling us.

What does Squarespace cost long-term?

Squarespace runs $192–$588/year ($16–$49/month) plus your time. Over 5 years that’s $960–$2,940. Plus the cost of features you’ll need to add (Acuity, e-commerce upgrade, custom domain, etc.). The total often crosses $3,000+ over 5 years for a real working setup.

Can I move off Squarespace later?

You can export content but you can’t export the design or the integrations. A move off Squarespace usually means rebuilding from scratch. Little Fight builds you the site you own — host it anywhere, edit anywhere, leave us anytime. No lock-in.

What does Little Fight do that Squarespace can’t?

Custom design (not template), real integrations with your POS or booking system, owner-trainable so you can edit yourself, and a real human who picks up when something breaks. Squarespace is software — Little Fight is software plus a tech partner.

Is Squarespace ever the right answer?

Yes. If you have one product, no integrations, time to learn the platform, and the site doesn’t have to do much beyond exist — Squarespace is fine. We’ll tell you that honestly. The first hour of consulting is free; we’ll point you at Squarespace if that’s the right call.
