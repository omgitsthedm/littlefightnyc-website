# Little Fight NYC Client Proof Collection

Last updated: 2026-07-20

## Rule

Publish only proof the client has approved and Little Fight can trace to a source. A good case study can be specific without a testimonial. Never turn an internal observation into a client quote.

## Proof record

Create one record per client with:

- client and approving contact;
- approved name, role, company, photo, and logo usage;
- exact quote as approved, with no cleanup after approval;
- the before-state baseline;
- the after-state result;
- measurement source, owner, and date range;
- launch date and public URL;
- permission scope: website, proposal, social, or all;
- approval date and a link to the written approval;
- expiration or review date, if the client requests one.

## Questions that produce useful evidence

Ask only the questions relevant to the work:

1. What was hardest before this project?
2. What changed in the first month after launch?
3. What now takes less time or fewer steps?
4. Are calls, bookings, form submissions, or qualified leads easier to track?
5. What did Little Fight keep instead of replacing?
6. What would you tell another owner considering the same work?
7. May we publish your answer with your name, role, company, photo, and logo?

## Measurement worksheet

| Field | Required entry |
| --- | --- |
| Claim | One plain sentence |
| Baseline | Value and date range before launch |
| Result | Value and date range after launch |
| Source | Analytics report, booking system, business system, invoice, or client-approved statement |
| Owner | Person who can verify the source |
| Caveat | Seasonality, campaign, partial tracking, or another factor |
| Approval | Link to written client approval |

If there is no reliable baseline, publish an implementation fact instead: launch time, performance audit, tools connected, steps removed, or production status.

## Approval request draft

Subject: Quick approval for your Little Fight case study

We are tightening the proof on the Little Fight NYC website and want to keep your project accurate. Could you answer the questions below in your own words? We will send the exact quote, name, role, and any image back for written approval before anything is published.

1. What was hardest before the project?
2. What is easier now?
3. Is there one result we can measure or state plainly?
4. May we use your name, role, company, photo, and logo on the case study?

No response is treated as approval. Save the reply and the final approval with the proof record.
