Why custom outperforms templates

It’s not just price — it’s the customers you lose.

The lifetime cost above is just dollars. The bigger cost is what templates do to your SEO ranking, conversion rate, and the customers you never get. Here’s the gap.

Google ranks faster sites higher

Custom sites: Lighthouse 95–100. Template sites: 50–75. The gap directly affects ranking. A faster custom site with the same SEO fundamentals will outrank a template site for the same keywords, every time.

Templates compete with thousands of clones

If you picked Squarespace template “Brine,” ~50,000 other small businesses also did. Google sees structural similarity. Custom = unique signal Google can rank.

JavaScript bloat kills conversion

Template platforms ship 1.5–3 MB of editor JavaScript on every page. Custom sites ship 50–200 KB. The 3-second slower load costs you 30%+ of your visitors before they see your offer.

Generic schema = generic search results

Templates ship one site-wide schema block. Custom sites ship page-specific JSON-LD: Service, FAQPage, LocalBusiness, Article. That’s how AI search (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI) cites you instead of the chain.

Mobile-first is not the same as “mobile-friendly”

Templates are typically desktop-first with mobile bolted on. NYC small business traffic is 70%+ mobile. Custom sites are designed mobile-first — the site looks and converts better on the phone where your customers actually are.

Conversion is designed, not chosen from a dropdown

Template platforms give you a hero, a few sections, a contact form. Custom sites are designed around how YOUR specific customers buy — the menu, the booking, the local pickup, the Fit Check. That’s 2–5× conversion.

The honest part

When a template platform IS the right call.

We’ll tell you straight: not every NYC small business needs a custom site. If you’re solo, no integrations, no growth plan, and you have time to learn the editor — Squarespace or Wix is fine. Don’t pay us for that. Use the calculator above to confirm the math, then go DIY.

But if your site has to actually convert customers, has to integrate with a POS or booking system, has to rank on Google for competitive keywords, or has to be owned outright — the template platform is the wrong tool. The calculator above shows you why.
