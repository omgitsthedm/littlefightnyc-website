# Little Fight NYC Design Language

Source of truth as of May 7, 2026:

`/Users/davidmarsh/Downloads/The base of Little Fight NYC.zip`

This zip is the default visual language for the scratch rebuild. Use it as the design reference, not the older production site, not the prior generic scaffold, and not any Leadbolt/Kimi placeholder strategy inside the archive.

## Core Feel

Little Fight NYC should feel like a premium New York systems workshop for local businesses.

It should be:

- Dark, cinematic, and warm at first glance
- Orange-led, with animated orange dots/particles as the signature atmosphere
- Practical and visual enough for a tired owner to understand fast
- Agency-capable without sounding corporate
- Neighborhood-aware without tourist gimmicks
- Clear enough for non-technical and non-native-English readers

## Copy Rules

Lead with owner outcomes, not technical labels.

Use:

- Money
- Time
- Customers
- Broken things
- Calls, bookings, forms, payments, Google, email
- Plain English

Avoid leading with:

- APIs
- AEO
- CRM
- schema
- automation
- digital transformation
- consulting abstractions

Technical terms can appear only after the plain-English value is clear.

## Four Service Areas

Every important page should make these four areas easy to see:

1. Websites
2. IT Support
3. Local Search
4. Business Systems

The site should make clear that Little Fight is one partner for the digital side of a local business, not four disconnected service menus.

## Non-Negotiables

- Keep the midnight blue/orange atmosphere.
- Use visuals early, not only text.
- Keep the four service areas visible.
- Make CTAs feel like help, not pressure.
- Do not import the base zip's old Leadbolt positioning.
- Do not add unsupported stats.
- Do not bloat the app with unnecessary animation libraries unless the page truly needs them.
