# Off-Domain Playbook — Little Fight NYC

> The website is the conversion engine. This doc covers everything OUTSIDE the
> website that helps it pay off. Updated to reflect channels David actually
> wants to run (no YouTube, no Reddit).

**Last updated:** May 6, 2026
**Owner:** David Marsh
**Time commitment:** 1–2 hours per week sustained.

---

## The honest picture

The May 2026 AEO research said YouTube and Reddit are the two biggest AI-search
citation sources. David's answer: not on the table. So we're playing a
different game — one that fits how Little Fight actually wins clients:

- **Referrals** — most of David's clients come through word-of-mouth from
  prior projects. The website needs to be a referral-closer, not a top-of-funnel
  awareness machine.
- **Google Business Profile + reviews** — when someone Googles "Little Fight NYC"
  or "[neighborhood] IT support," GBP is the first impression.
- **Direct outreach** — when David spots a problem (a broken site, a suspended
  GBP, a salon paying $400/mo for booking they don't use), a thoughtful email
  or call wins business that referrals never would.
- **Press + listicle inclusion** — passive but compounding. Once you're in a
  "Best NYC small business IT" listicle, it works for years.

This is enough. It won't make Little Fight a viral brand — but it doesn't need
to be. It needs to be the obvious referral when a NYC owner asks "who does
my friend's website?"

## The four channels David will actually run

### 1. Google Business Profile — the foundation

**Why first:** when someone Googles "Little Fight NYC" or "NYC small business
IT support," GBP is the first impression after the homepage. It also feeds
local pack and AI Overviews for local queries.

**Goal:** GBP fully optimized. Steady review trickle. Weekly posts.

**Setup checklist:**
- [ ] Verify GBP listing is claimed and fully filled
- [ ] Categories: "IT Service," "Web Designer," "Computer Consultant" (max 10)
- [ ] All hours, holiday hours, services field filled in detail
- [ ] 8–12 real photos: David working, real client shops (with permission),
      screen captures of dashboards built, before/after if visual
- [ ] Service area set: Manhattan + outer boroughs you actually serve

**Weekly cadence (15 min/week, total):**
- Post once a week: a "Fires of the Week" snippet, a service highlight, or
  a tip. Recycle from `/fires/` content.
- After every closed engagement: ask the client for a Google review.

**Reviews:** target 1 per closed engagement. With 20+ NYC businesses since
2017, even one new review per month transforms local visibility over a year.
A short email template lives in this repo's `/scripts/` folder if you want
me to draft one.

**Effort:** 15 min/week + 5 min/closed engagement. That's it.

---

### 2. Direct outreach — the referral compounder

**Why second:** David's strongest channel. Most clients come through prior
work. Direct outreach when you spot a problem (the law firm whose WiFi went
down last week, the boutique whose website looks 10 years old) converts
much higher than cold lists, because it's specific.

**Format:**
- See a broken site / GBP / setup → send a 3-sentence email or DM, no pitch:
  "Saw [specific problem]. Common fix is [X], usually 1–2 hours. If it's
  costing you revenue, happy to help — David at Little Fight NYC."
- Don't list services. Don't link to the website (let them Google it if curious).
- One specific observation > a generic pitch.

**Tracking:**
- Keep a running spreadsheet: business, problem spotted, message sent, response.
- Goal: 3–5 outreach messages per week. Most won't reply. The ones that do
  are usually pre-qualified.

**Effort:** 30 min/week.

---

### 3. Press + listicle inclusion — passive compounding

**Why third:** once you're in a "Best NYC small business IT" or "Top NYC web
designers" listicle, it works for years. Compounds slowly but quietly.

**Targets:**
- *amNewYork Metro* — small business features
- *The City NYC* — neighborhood business stories
- *Crain's New York Business* — small biz tech coverage
- *Built In NYC* — tech ecosystem listings
- *Brooklyn Daily Eagle / Brownstoner* — Brooklyn coverage
- *Patch* (neighborhood-level)

**Listicles:**
- "Best NYC IT services for small businesses" — most are paid placements,
  but some are organic. Search "best IT services NYC small business 2026"
  and reach out to organic ones.
- "Best web designers NYC" — same playbook.

**The pitch:** one short email per outlet. Real angle, not a press release.
"Hi — I run Little Fight NYC. Helped 20+ NYC businesses since 2017. Recently
[specific story: rescued a salon's GBP, rebuilt a law firm's WiFi mid-emergency].
If you ever cover NYC small business tech, I'm happy to be a source." Then
shut up.

**Targets:** 1–2 outreach emails per month. Enough to land 2–3 hits/year.

**Effort:** 30 min/month, mostly drafting.

---

### 4. Existing client touchpoints — the relationship engine

**Why fourth:** the cheapest revenue is from clients who already know you.
Re-engagement, expansion work, referrals.

**Cadence:**
- **Quarterly check-in email** to every client from the last 3 years. Two
  sentences: "Hey, checking in — how's the [thing we built/fixed] holding up?
  Any tech that's been bothering you?" That's it. Most won't reply. The ones
  who do are warm leads.
- **Referral ask** at the end of every closed engagement: "If you know any
  NYC business owners who'd benefit from this, mind passing along my number?
  No pressure."

**Effort:** 1 hour/quarter for the check-in batch. 30 seconds per closed
engagement.

## The honest caveats

Without YouTube/Reddit, **AI search citations will grow slowly** — they're
the two channels that move that needle fastest in May 2026. Little Fight
will get cited mainly when:
- Someone searches "Little Fight NYC" directly (referrals + GBP feed this)
- Someone hits an answer page from a long-tail query (the website does
  this directly)
- A press hit gets indexed by ChatGPT/Perplexity (slower, but real)

That's enough for a referral-driven NYC services business. It's not enough
to make Little Fight a category brand. That's a fair trade.
