## Why this matters

Per the May 2026 AEO research:
- **Brand search volume is the strongest single predictor of AI citations** (0.334 correlation, n=1.2M citations — Kevin Indig, Feb 2026).
- **YouTube overtook Reddit as the #1 AI citation source in Q1 2026** (~16% vs ~10%).
- For "best NYC IT company" type queries, the cited source is often a YouTube video or Reddit thread — **not anyone's website**.

**Translation:** if nobody Googles "Little Fight NYC" and there's nothing on YouTube/Reddit/podcasts/press about you, the website overhaul won't compound. The on-site work and off-domain work are a single system.

## The four channels, in priority order

### 1. YouTube — highest-leverage, biggest commitment

**Why first:** #1 AI citation source as of Q1 2026. Even small channels get cited for niche queries (NYC small business tech is niche). Each video is a citation magnet that lives forever.

**Goal:** 4–6 videos in the first 60 days. Then 1 per week minimum.

**Format suggestions** (you on camera, real, not over-produced):
- *"Fires of the Week"* — 5-10 minute weekly video walking through the fires you put out. Same content as `/fires/` but in your voice.
- *"WTF is broken in NYC small business tech this month"* — monthly, ~15 minutes, 3-5 specific common issues with specific advice.
- *"Square vs Toast for a NYC bar/restaurant"* — head-to-head, specific to NYC scale.
- *"Why your $400/month booking software is robbing you"* — opinionated, specific.
- *"On-site walkthrough: a real NYC shop's tech stack audit"* — anonymized client visit (with their permission).

**Setup checklist:**
- [ ] Create channel `@LittleFightNYC` (or similar)
- [ ] Channel banner + description (I can help draft)
- [ ] Video 1 published in first 7 days
- [ ] Each video has: title with NYC + topic, description with link to relevant `/quadrant/`, pinned comment with Fit Check link
- [ ] Each video gets 1 short (vertical 60s clip) for YouTube Shorts + Instagram + TikTok if you want

**Effort:** 2 hours per video at first. Drops to 1 hour once a setup exists.

### 2. Reddit — earned mentions in real conversations

**Why second:** ~10% of AI citations across all major engines, even after Reddit/Perplexity dispute (Reddit citation share dropped 86% on Perplexity overnight in October 2025 — don't put all eggs here, but it still matters for ChatGPT/Google AI).

**Goal:** genuine, helpful presence in 3-5 subreddits. NOT spam.

**Subreddits to be present in:**
- `r/AskNYC` — general NYC questions, lots of "any recommendations for X" threads
- `r/NYCsmallbusiness` (if exists) or `r/smallbusiness`
- `r/Entrepreneur` — niche-specific advice when relevant
- Industry subs that match your client base: `r/Restaurateur`, `r/Salons`, `r/sysadmin` (for IT-flavored answers)
- Borough/neighborhood subs: `r/Manhattan`, `r/Brooklyn`, etc.

**Rules:**
- Use a real, named account. Bio: "David — runs Little Fight NYC, helps small businesses with tech."
- 80% genuine helpful answers / 20% mention of own work (only when truly relevant)
- Never paste the URL in the first reply. Mention "I run a small NYC shop that does this" — let people DM if interested.
- Mods will ban for self-promo. Be useful first.

**Setup checklist:**
- [ ] Reddit username (suggest: `littlefightnyc` or `dmarsh-nyc` — your call)
- [ ] Bio + flair set up
- [ ] 5 helpful comments in first week (no self-mention)
- [ ] Then 1-2 helpful comments per week + occasional original posts

**Effort:** 30 minutes per week.

### 3. Google Business Profile — local pack + AI Overview foundation

**Why third:** GBP is the primary data source AI systems use for local citations. Review velocity matters as much as total count. Local-pack queries increasingly fold into AI Overviews directly.

**Goal:** 1-3 reviews/week sustained. Weekly post. Real photos.

**Setup checklist:**
- [ ] Verify GBP listing is claimed and fully filled
- [ ] Categories: "IT Service", "Web Designer", "Computer Consultant" (max 10 categories)
- [ ] All hours, holiday hours, services field filled in detail
- [ ] 8-12 real photos: storefront if you have one, you working, real client shops (with permission), screen captures of dashboards you've built
- [ ] Service area set: Manhattan + outer boroughs you actually serve

**Weekly cadence** (template):
- **Monday:** post a "Fires of the Week" snippet (1 paragraph, photo if you have one, link to `/fires/`)
- **Wednesday:** post a service highlight (one of the 4 quadrants, rotating)
- **Friday:** post a "before/after" or a tip (one specific thing you fixed, no client name)

**Reviews:**
- After every closed engagement: ask the client for a Google review.
- Email template (I can draft) with a direct link to your review form.
- Aim 1-3/week. 30+ reviews in 90 days transforms local rankings.

**Effort:** 30 min/week for posts, 10 min/client for review request.

### 4. Press / Listicles / Podcasts — long-tail brand mentions

**Why fourth:** brand mention frequency across the web feeds AI citation. One good NYC press hit > 50 SEO backlinks for AEO purposes.

**Goal:** 2-3 brand mentions per quarter in venues NYC small business owners read.

**NYC venues to target** (manual outreach, I can write the emails):
- *amNewYork Metro* — small business features
- *The City NYC* — neighborhood business stories
- *Crain's New York Business*
- *Brooklyn Daily Eagle / Brownstoner* — Brooklyn coverage
- *Bedford + Bowery* — East Village/Williamsburg
- *Patch* (neighborhood-level)

**Listicles to be in:**
- "Best NYC IT services for small businesses" — most are paid placements, but some are organic. Find them via Google "best IT services NYC small business 2026" and reach out.
- "Best web designers in NYC" — same playbook.
- *Built In NYC* tech ecosystem listings.

**Podcasts to pitch:**
- *Smart Passive Income* (if relevant)
- *NYC small business focused podcasts* (search NYC + small business + podcast)
- *Industry-specific*: restaurant, salon, retail podcasts that talk to NYC owners

**Outreach effort:** 1-2 hours per week, 90% emails I draft and you send.

## Questions you'll have

**Q: Do I have to be on camera?**
A: For the YouTube channel to do its job — yes. People search for *people*, not faceless brands. A founder-led channel where you talk plainly is what gets cited. We can keep videos short and unscripted.

**Q: What if Reddit bans me for self-promo?**
A: Lead with help, not promotion. Mention Little Fight only when directly relevant ("I run a small NYC shop that does this kind of work — happy to answer questions"). Don't paste URLs in first replies.

**Q: How do I balance this with paying client work?**
A: Block 2 hours every Friday morning. Treat it like a recurring meeting. Skip a week and the compounding stalls.

**Q: When will I see results?**
A: GBP reviews and weekly posts → local pack lift in 30 days. Reddit + listicles → brand mentions in 60 days. YouTube + AI citations → real visible traction in 90-120 days. The brand-search flywheel takes 6 months to feel automatic.
