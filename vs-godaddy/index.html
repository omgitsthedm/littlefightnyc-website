When each wins

Honest read on which is right for you.

Pick GoDaddy if:

  • You only need a domain registration (they’re fine for that)
  • You just need a placeholder page that says “we exist”
  • You don’t care if it converts customers
  • You’re willing to do all the work yourself, indefinitely
  • You’re OK with bad support when something breaks

Pick Little Fight if:

  • The site has to actually win customers
  • You want a real human to call when something breaks
  • You want to own the site outright — no platform lock-in
  • You’ve been burned by GoDaddy before
  • You want to know the real total cost before you commit

Owner FAQ

Questions owners ask first.

Should a NYC small business use GoDaddy or hire Little Fight?

GoDaddy wins on price floor — $9.99/month for the basic builder, $499+ for Pro Web Design. Little Fight wins on everything else: real custom design (not a template), real human you can call, real ownership of the site, and a real result that converts customers. GoDaddy is a price floor, not a quality floor.

Why do NYC owners regret GoDaddy?

Three reasons we hear most often: (1) the support is famously bad — long hold times, scripted responses, no continuity; (2) the design templates are dated and look like every other GoDaddy site; (3) the upsells never stop — domain privacy, SSL, email, security, hosting tier upgrades, all sold separately. The "cheap" site ends up costing more than expected within 2 years.

What’s GoDaddy Pro Web Design?

GoDaddy’s done-for-you website service starting at $499. They use offshore designers working from templates. The result is a generic site that exists, but rarely converts. There’s no relationship — you submit feedback through a portal, you get revisions, the site gets pushed live. No founder calls, no ongoing partnership, no real understanding of your shop.

How is Little Fight different?

Custom-designed (not a template). David picks up the phone (no portal, no offshore handoff). Live in 14 days, or you don’t pay. You own the site outright — no platform lock-in. Clients save 50% on average compared to NYC agency quotes for the same work.

Is GoDaddy ever the right answer?

For domain registration only — GoDaddy is fine. For an actual working website that wins customers? Almost never the right call for a NYC small business. We’d rather see you on Squarespace than GoDaddy if you’re DIY’ing.
